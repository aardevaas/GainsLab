## 4. Competitive Landscape  **[CLAUDE — to expand via competitive-teardown]**

Where the sharks win, and — more importantly — where they leave the door open.

| Competitor | Wins at | Fails at (your opening) | Price |
|------------|---------|-------------------------|-------|
| **MyFitnessPal** | Largest food DB, brand ubiquity | Aggressive paywalls, ad-heavy, weak training side, dated UX, no real science | ~$20/mo premium |
| **Cronometer** | Micronutrient accuracy, data-rich | Niche/clinical feel, weak training & community, not motivating | ~$10/mo |
| **Hevy / Strong** | Best-in-class workout logging | Nutrition is absent or an afterthought; no body/education layer | ~$5–10/mo |
| **Fitbod** | AI workout generation | No nutrition, no community, no progress depth | ~$13/mo |
| **Macrofactor** | Smart adaptive macro coaching | Expensive, nutrition-only, no training/community | ~$12/mo |
| **Whoop / Oura** | Recovery/biometrics | Hardware-gated, no training/nutrition logging | $$$ + device |

**The pattern:** every competitor is **deep in one lane and absent in the
others.** Nobody owns the *whole athlete* — train + eat + measure + learn +
compete — in one coherent product. That's the wedge.
