# Competitive Teardown

> Five competitors, chosen as the best-in-class of each lane: **MyFitnessPal**
> (nutrition), **Cronometer** (nutrition depth), **Hevy** (workout logging),
> **Fitbod** (AI training), **Strava** (community/social). Pricing & figures
> verified via web research (Feb–Jun 2026 sources: app store listings, Sensor
> Tower, pricing pages, review aggregators). Treat revenue/download numbers as
> third-party estimates.

**The one-line takeaway:** every competitor is *elite in one lane and absent in
the others.* No one owns the whole athlete — **train + eat + measure + learn +
compete** — in one coherent, fairly-priced product. That gap is GainsLab's wedge.

---

## 1. MyFitnessPal — the dethroned king (Nutrition)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Owns** | Largest food database (~14M+ entries), ubiquitous brand, integrations with nearly every wearable (Garmin, Fitbit, Apple Health, Withings…) |
| **Price** | Premium $19.99/mo · $79.99/yr · Premium+ $24.99/mo · $99.99/yr |
| **Scale** | Category incumbent; owned by Francisco Partners since 2020 |

**Biggest weakness (the opening):** trust collapse from aggressive monetization.
The **2024 barcode-scanner paywall** triggered a backlash ("The death of
MyFitnessPal," 292↑ on Reddit). UI is cluttered with ads/upsells, dated, and
user-submitted food entries have **quality/accuracy problems**. Training side is
an afterthought. Widely described in 2026 reviews as "once-king, now dethroned."

**GainsLab vs. MFP:** *"Everything you log MFP for — without the ads, the
paywalled basics, or the junk data — plus the training and progress side MFP
never built."*

**Response risk: LOW.** Slow, PE-owned, harvesting an existing base. Unlikely to
out-innovate; their moat is inertia, not delight.

---

## 2. Cronometer — the accuracy specialist (Nutrition depth)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Owns** | Lab-verified, USDA/NCCDB food data; **84-micronutrient** tracking; the most *trusted* numbers in the category; "Oracle" nutrient-gap suggestions |
| **Price** | Gold $8.99/mo · $54.99/yr (raised in early 2026); Pro $39.99/mo |
| **Scale** | Profitable, respected; Huberman-adjacent credibility |

**Biggest weakness (the opening):** clinical, joyless, solo. It's a spreadsheet
that tracks you superbly but doesn't *motivate, train, or connect* you. Free tier
crippled to a **7-day data window**. No workout side, no community, weak habit
loop. Recent price increase opened a value gap.

**GainsLab vs. Cronometer:** *"Cronometer-grade nutrition honesty, but with the
training, progress, and community that actually keep you coming back — and a
free tier that isn't a 7-day trial in disguise."*

**Response risk: LOW–MEDIUM.** Competent and principled, but deliberately
narrow. Expanding into training/community is off-brand for them.

---

## 3. Hevy — the beloved logger (Workouts)  ⚠️ closest to our DNA

| | |
|---|---|
| **Owns** | Best-in-class lifting log — clean UX, PRs, muscle-distribution, routines; a **genuinely generous free tier**; cult-loved (4.9★, ~192K reviews) |
| **Price** | Pro **$2.99/mo · $23.99/yr · $74.99 lifetime** — aggressively cheap |
| **Scale** | ~400K downloads/mo, ~$800K/mo revenue (Sensor Tower); Spain, est. 2019 |

**Biggest weakness (the opening):** lifting-only. **No nutrition, no body/
progress depth beyond lifts, no education.** A Hevy user still needs a separate
macro app, a separate progress app, a separate recipe app.

**GainsLab vs. Hevy:** *"Hevy-quality logging is table stakes — but your lifts,
your food, your body, and your progress finally live in one app, for about what
you'd pay for Hevy alone."*

**Response risk: MEDIUM–HIGH.** This is the dangerous one. Fast, loved, cheap,
shipping. If they add nutrition, they're the all-in-one. **Implication:** our
logging must at least match Hevy, and we must move on the all-in-one wedge
before they do. Their low price also **anchors the market** — we can't price far
above ~$5–8/mo for the lifting-centric persona.

---

## 4. Fitbod — the AI trainer (Workouts / intelligence)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Owns** | Adaptive AI workout generation; **per-muscle recovery scoring (0–100%)**; equipment/time flexibility; Apple Editor's Choice |
| **Price** | $15.99/mo · $95.99/yr (~$8/mo); 7-day trial |
| **Scale** | ~200K downloads/mo, ~$1M/mo revenue (Sensor Tower); US, est. 2015 |

**Biggest weakness (the opening):** training-only and **pricey for one lane**.
No nutrition, no community, shallow long-term progress analytics. You pay
$96/yr for workout generation alone. Recovery model is muscle-only — it doesn't
see your *nutrition or sleep*, which actually drive recovery.

**GainsLab vs. Fitbod:** *"Fitbod guesses your recovery from your lifts alone.
GainsLab knows it — because it also sees what you ate and how you slept — and
costs less while doing five other jobs."*

**Response risk: MEDIUM.** Smart and well-funded, but committed to the
training-only identity; nutrition/community is a big pivot for them.

---

## 5. Strava — the social engine (Community)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Owns** | The **social/competitive layer** of fitness — segments, KOM/QOM leaderboards, kudos, clubs, the best route builder; genuine network effects |
| **Price** | Subscription ~$11.99/mo · ~$79.99/yr (controversial opaque regional pricing since the 2022 hike) |
| **Scale** | The category's social default; 100M+ users (mostly endurance/cycling/run) |

**Biggest weakness (the opening):** **endurance-centric and not a training or
nutrition tool.** It's where you *share* a run/ride, not where you *plan
training, lift, or eat*. Strength athletes are second-class. Pulling core
features (full leaderboards) behind the paywall bred resentment; free Garmin
Connect / Komoot pressure it.

**GainsLab vs. Strava:** *"Strava made cardio social. GainsLab makes your *whole*
fitness life social — lifting, nutrition consistency, body progress — for the
gym crowd Strava ignores."*

**Response risk: LOW–MEDIUM.** Huge but firmly endurance/social. Strength +
nutrition + community is not their lane; the network effect protects *them*, not
their ability to enter *our* space.

## Synthesis — where GainsLab attacks

### The white space (no competitor occupies all of this)
1. **The whole athlete in one app** — train + eat + measure + learn + compete,
   with the data *connected* (nutrition informs recovery informs training).
2. **The connected-data moat** — Fitbod can't see your food; MFP can't see your
   training; Strava sees neither for lifters. GainsLab sees all of it, which
   compounds: better recommendations the more you use it.
3. **Honest, generous free tier** — the whole category is paywalling basics
   (MFP barcode, Cronometer's 7-day window). Generosity is a wedge.
4. **The gym/strength crowd that Strava abandoned** — social fitness built for
   lifters, not just cyclists.

### Pricing map (what we're priced against)
| App | Effective $/mo (annual) | Lane |
|-----|------------------------|------|
| Hevy | ~$2 | Workouts |
| Cronometer | ~$4.50–8 | Nutrition |
| Fitbod | ~$8 | Workouts (AI) |
| Strava | ~$6.50 | Community |
| MyFitnessPal | ~$6.67 | Nutrition |
| **A user buying the stack** | **~$25–30/mo** | (3–5 apps) |

**The price story writes itself:** one GainsLab subscription replaces a
$25–30/mo stack. Even at $9.99–12.99/mo it's a *discount* on the status quo while
being our highest-margin tier. Hevy anchors the floor (~$5), so the
all-in-one value must be unmistakable to justify a premium over it.

### Draft positioning statement *(for the strategy doc — refine with your vision)*
> *"Five apps' worth of fitness — training, nutrition, body tracking, education,
> and community — in one science-backed platform, for less than you pay for two
> of them today. Built for people who lift and eat with intent, not just runners
> with a cardio feed."*

### Sharpest attack vectors, ranked
1. **All-in-one + connected data** (the structural moat none can copy quickly).
2. **Price-to-replace-the-stack** (one bill vs. five).
3. **Generous free tier** (against a paywall-fatigued market).
4. **Strength-first community** (Strava's abandoned flank).
5. **Science/education trust layer** (where MFP's junk-data reputation is weakest).

### Biggest threat to watch
**Hevy.** Loved, cheap, fast, and one nutrition module away from being the
all-in-one themselves. Our logging must match theirs, and we must plant the
all-in-one flag first.
